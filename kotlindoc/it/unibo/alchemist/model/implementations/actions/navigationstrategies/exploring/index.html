Exploring

open class Exploring<T, N, E> : EuclideanNavigationStrategy<T, N, E, ConvexPolygon, Euclidean2DPassage>, Any

A NavigationStrategy allowing to explore the environment. In order to choose which direction to take, a weighting system is used: every time the pedestrian enters a new room all the visible doors are weighted, the one with minimum weight is then crossed. The weighting system used here is derived from the one by Andresen et al., see weight.

weight

protected open fun weight(door: Euclidean2DPassage): Double

Assigns a weight to a visible door (= passage). This weighting system is derived from the one by Andresen et al.. By default, it comprises three factors: volatileMemoryFactor, congestionFactor and impasseFactor.

volatileMemoryFactor

protected open fun volatileMemoryFactor(head: ConvexPolygon): Double

Takes into account the information stored in the pedestrian's volatile memory. It is computed as 2^v where v is the number of visits to the area the edge being weighted leads to (in other words, less visited rooms are preferred).

congestionFactor

protected open fun congestionFactor(head: ConvexPolygon): Double

Takes into account the congestion of the area the edge being weighted leads to (it is assumed that the pedestrian can estimate the congestion level of a neighboring room). It is computed as density of the area + 1, so as to have a value in 1,2 (less crowded rooms are preferred).

impasseFactor

protected open fun impasseFactor(head: ConvexPolygon): Double

Takes into account whereas the assessed edge leads to a known impasse or not, known impasses are given knownImpasseWeight (which is usually a high value, allowing to avoid them), otherwise this factor assumes unitary value.
